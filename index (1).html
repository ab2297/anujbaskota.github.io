Anuj Baskota

PhD Student · Biomedical Engineering · Cornell University

I develop imaging protocols at the intersection of high-frequency acoustics, optics, and microfluidics — using GHz ultrasound and multiphoton microscopy to visualize biological and physical phenomena at micro- and nanoscale resolution.

My current work focuses on intravital multiphoton imaging of tumor immunology (Xu Lab, Cornell) and on integrating GHz ultrasonic systems with optical and mechanical modalities for next-generation bioimaging platforms.

Research

Current Projects

Multiphoton Imaging of Tumor Immunology

Developing 2-photon and 3-photon intravital microscopy protocols to study immune-oncology interactions in living tissue. Conducted in the Xu Lab at Cornell, this work enables deep-tissue, high-resolution visualization of immune cell behavior and tumor microenvironments with minimal photodamage.

Image / GIF placeholder

GHz Ultrasonic Imaging of C. elegans

Real-time GHz ultrasonic imaging of the nematode C. elegans at microscopic resolution, enabling non-invasive visualization and mechanical characterization of cuticle stiffness, morphology, and aging. Acoustic impedance modeling reveals age-dependent biomechanical changes inaccessible to optical methods alone.

IUS 2024 M&M 2023 IUS 2022 M&M 2022 IUS 2021

GHz Ultrasonics with Optical & Mechanical Integration

Integrating GHz ultrasonic transducer arrays with optical and mechanical sensing modalities to build compact multi-modal imagers for cells, tissues, and materials. Applications span medical diagnostics, hydrogel mechanobiology, semiconductor metrology, flexible electronics inspection, and microfluidic imaging.

MEMS 2024 MEMS 2024 Transducers 2023 IUS 2023 IUS 2022 MEMS 2022

Droplet & Bubble Dynamics

Experimental and theoretical study of bubble and droplet mechanics at fluid interfaces — including backflipping bubble collisions with tilted walls and the Rayleigh–Taylor instability governing water ejection from the ear canal. This work combines high-speed imaging, fabricated PDMS micromodels, and analytical fluid mechanics.

arXiv (under review) J. Fluid Mech. 2023
